The spectra of curl operators on a 3-torus. Summer 2016
Consider a $3$-torus of $2\pi$-periodity in $x^1$, $x^2$ and $x^3$ endowed with the Riemmannian metric $$g_{\alpha\beta}= \begin{pmatrix} 1&0&0\\ 0&1+\epsilon\cos x^1&\epsilon\sin x^1\\ 0&\epsilon\sin x^1&1-\epsilon\cos x^1 \end{pmatrix}^2.$$ A previous research paper says that the axisymmetric massless Dirac operator working on half-densities \begin{multline} W_{1/2}(\epsilon)= -\frac i2 \begin{pmatrix} e_3{}^1&e_1{}^1-ie_2{}^1 \\ e_1{}^1+ie_2{}^1&-e_3{}^1 \end{pmatrix} \frac d{d x^1} -\frac i2 \frac d{d x^1} \begin{pmatrix} e_3{}^1&e_1{}^1-ie_2{}^1 \\ e_1{}^1+ie_2{}^1&-e_3{}^1 \end{pmatrix} \\ + \frac{\delta_{jk}}{4\sqrt{\det g_{\alpha\beta}}} \left[ e^j{}_{3} \left(\frac{d e^k{}_{2}}{d x^1}\right) - e^j{}_{2} \left(\frac{d e^k{}_{3}}{d x^1}\right) \right]I \end{multline} obtains explicit solutions of eigenvalues and eigenfunctions as cited in Downes, RJ; Levitin, M; Vassiliev, D; (2013) Spectral asymmetry of the massless Dirac operator on a 3-torus. JOURNAL OF MATHEMATICAL PHYSICS, 54 (11), 111503. Explicit solutions are of a rare phenomenon with respect to the generality of metric tensor, which has its speciality in this case. It becomes curious to investigate whether the curl operator on the torus with the same Riemmanian metric shares the property for its spectrum to obtain explicit solutions.

We wrote down explicitly the axisymmetric curl operator on our torus $$ \operatorname{curl}_{1/2}(\epsilon)=\begin{pmatrix} 0 & 0 & 0\\ 0 & 0 & -1\\ 0 & 1 & 0 \end{pmatrix}\frac{d}{d x^1} -(1-\epsilon^2)^{-1}\epsilon\begin{pmatrix} 0 & 0 & 0\\ 0 & \epsilon+\cos x^1 & \sin x^1\\ 0 & \sin x^1 & \epsilon-\cos x^1 \end{pmatrix}, $$ which works on half-densities. We have shown it has eigenvalues in their explicit forms $$ \lambda^{n}=-\frac12-\frac{\epsilon^2}{1-\epsilon^2}\pm\sqrt{n^2-n+\frac14+\frac{\epsilon^2}{(1-\epsilon^2)^2}} $$ for $n\in\mathbb{N}$ together with corresponding explicit eigenfuctions. This result consolidates our conjecture that on such metric all reasonable half-density operators (curl, Laplacian and Dirac) attain explict axisymmetric solutions of eigenvalue problems. We are currently working on further inspections upon this conjecture.
